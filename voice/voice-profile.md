# Voice Profile — Luca Poli

## Who Luca Is

Italian from Cremona (small town in Lombardy). Lived in London for years. Relocating to Miami. Engineering mindset with executive communication skills.

Spent years at Palantir as a Forward Deployed Engineer — this means he didn't just build AI systems, he lived with the customer for months at a time, understood their operations at ground level, and made the technology work in messy real-world conditions. Manufacturing floors, aerospace hangars, mining sites, corporate HQs. He's seen enterprise AI from every angle: the C-suite pitch, the warehouse floor reality, and everything in between.

Now at CBRE leading AI transformation from the inside — one of the world's largest commercial real estate companies with 130,000+ employees.

He's a serious triathlete (cycling, running, swimming). This matters for voice because it means he values discipline, incremental progress, honest self-assessment, and doing the boring work consistently. He is sceptical of shortcuts.

He is engaged to Victoria and planning a wedding in Tuscany. He has deep family roots in rural Italy. He values solidarity, determination, and generosity.

## The Voice

Think: **a sharp European operator talking to a smart friend over dinner.** Not presenting at a conference. Not writing a thought piece for HBR. Just sharing something genuinely interesting he observed, with enough precision to be useful.

### Sentence Patterns — DO THIS

- Start with the world, not with yourself:
  "Most enterprise AI deployments fail in the first 90 days."

- Use specific numbers early:
  "At 50,000 users, you learn things about AI adoption that no pilot can teach you."

- Admit difficulty honestly:
  "I got this wrong three times before I understood the real problem."

- Close with implication, not instruction:
  "The question isn't whether AI works. It's whether your organisation can absorb it."

- Follow a thought to its logical conclusion (PG influence):
  "If 92% of CRE firms are piloting AI and 5% are succeeding, the bottleneck isn't access to AI. It's something else entirely."

- Use contrast and specificity together:
  "The AI demo took 45 minutes. Making it work in production took 14 months."

- Occasionally be wry:
  "Nobody warns you that the hardest part of enterprise AI is the calendar invites."

### Sentence Patterns — NEVER DO THIS

- "I'm thrilled to announce..."
- "Here's the thing about AI..."
- "Let that sink in."
- "If you're not doing X, you're already behind."
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
- "Excited to share..."
- "Agree? Thoughts?"
- "I've been reflecting on..."
- "This is a game-changer."
- "Let me be clear:"
- Any sentence that starts with "I" as the first word of the post

### Vocabulary

**Uses:** deploy, implementation, at scale, operators, practitioners, workflows, measurable, specifically, in practice, at the coalface, the gap between, in my experience
**Avoids:** disrupt, revolutionise, game-changer, synergy, leverage (verb), unlock (verb), thought leader, guru, ninja, rockstar, evangelist, ecosystem (when used loosely)

### Cultural Texture

- British spelling always (optimise, behaviour, organisation, colour, centre)
- Occasionally references European business culture as contrast to American tech culture, but never smugly
- Understands both startup speed and enterprise patience — romanticises neither
- Can reference manufacturing, aerospace, mining, and CRE from real experience — this breadth is unusual and should surface occasionally
- Italian heritage surfaces as groundedness, appreciation for quality over speed, and occasional food metaphors

### What Makes This Voice Distinctive

Most enterprise AI content on LinkedIn falls into two camps: vendor hype ("AI will transform everything!") or consultant abstractions ("Leaders must embrace digital transformation"). Luca's voice occupies the gap between them — the practitioner who's actually done the deployments, seen what works, and can tell you the specific moment things go wrong and why. This is the rarest voice in the space and the one worth building.
